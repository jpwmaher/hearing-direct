The most important differentiator for hearing aids is the type (ITE / BTE / RIC / CIC)

Most users were conscious about how their hearing aid looked.

For people with longer hair, BTE was discreet enough

Purchase decision is marriage of device type and price point, i.e. no one suggested invisible 'at all costs'

For NHS patients there hadn't been an option.

Customers understand the difference and pros and cons of each type of hearing aid
Users understood the aethestic differences in device type, but didn't seem to know of any pros/cons of each type, e.g. comfort, reliability, battery life
Price point is the main determinant as to which device within a type category is purchased

Purchase decision is marriage of device type and price point

People didn't want to spend more than needed

People generally didn't know of device & accessory costs

The ‘30 Day money back guarantee’ is a source of comfort to potential customers

The 30-day guarantee wasn't noticed often until user prompted.

Specsavers offer 90 day, Boots 60 day.

Time-pressure and lack of perceived customer service may trigger returns

"You have to get used to wearing them"

Some distrust about there being a catch with terms and conditions

Consider extending the period

User needs extra reassurance about customer service & support during the 'bedding in' period, especially for online purchase
